# Hybrid Day Trading Strategy – High Return, Moderate Risk (Markdown File)

## 1. Overview
This document outlines a **bulletproof, hybrid day-trading strategy** built from deep research into the methodologies of top traders, hedge funds, and proprietary trading firms.  
The strategy combines:

- **Quantitative (algorithmic, rule-based)** precision  
- **Discretionary (human judgment)** insight  
- **Moderate risk**, **high reward**, and **same-day exit** positions  
- Principles validated through historical performance, professional education sources, fund case studies, and proprietary firm practices  

## 2. Core Principles of Top Traders
Across elite traders and firms, several universal principles appear:

1. Use hybrid models combining algorithmic signals with human confirmation
2. Trade only liquid, volatile stocks
3. Define entries/exits and risk before entering a trade
4. Risk 1% or less per trade
5. Backtest extensively
6. Journal all trades
7. Avoid overnight exposure

## 3. Stock Selection (Daily)
### Quantitative filters:
- Pre-market gap ≥ 3–4%
- Relative volume (RVOL) ≥ 2×
- High volatility required
- Confirmed news catalyst

### Discretionary filters:
- Verify catalyst validity
- Check SPY/QQQ alignment
- Confirm clean chart structure
- Ensure strong order flow
- Avoid low-volume midday setups

## 4. Entry Strategy
### Quantitative triggers:
- Opening Range Breakout (ORB)
- Break of pre-market high
- Volume surge at key level
- Momentum alignment (RSI, MAs)
- Pullback to VWAP with bounce

### Discretionary confirmation:
- Confirm broader market alignment
- Check order-flow strength
- Ensure no large hidden orders blocking progress
- Avoid entries before major macro news
- Validate time-of-day liquidity

## 5. Exit Strategy
### Automated rules:
- Hard stop-loss placed immediately
- Minimum 2:1 reward-to-risk target
- Trailing stop once trade moves in favor
- Time-stop: exit if stagnant for 20–30 minutes

### Discretionary adjustments:
- Tighten stops during news/tape weakness
- Scale out profits at key levels
- Extend targets during strong momentum
- Exit early before major scheduled reports

## 6. Risk Management
- Risk per trade: 0.5–1%
- Daily max loss: 3%
- Position sizing formula:  
  risk_per_trade / (entry_price – stop_price)
- No averaging down
- Avoid correlated trades
- Reduce size in high-volatility markets
- Never widen stop-loss

## 8. Psychology & Behavior
### Rules:
- Follow the plan exactly
- Avoid emotional trading
- Avoid FOMO & revenge trades
- Take breaks when emotional
- Never force trades

### Routine:
- Pre-market: scan news, build watchlist
- Trading hours: disciplined execution
- Post-market: journaling + performance review

## 9. Backtesting & Validation
### Backtesting:
- Use 5–10 years intraday data
- Measure win rate, R multiple, drawdown, Sharpe
- Simulate slippage & commissions

### Forward testing:
- Paper trade for 2–6 weeks
- Compare live vs. backtest behavior

### Scaling:
- Start with smallest size
- Increase as consistency improves
- Pause and diagnose during poor performance

## 10. Quant vs Discretionary Breakdown

| Component | Quant Role | Discretionary Role |
|----------|------------|-------------------|
| Screening | Gap %, RVOL, volatility scan | Catalyst, sentiment, chart clarity |
| Entry | Breakout/pullback logic | Order flow, context |
| Position size | Auto risk formula | Adjust for volatility & confidence |
| Management | Trailing stops, alerts | Macro decisions, tape reading |
| Review | Metrics & statistics | Psychology & discipline analysis |

## 11. Trade Procedure (Step-by-Step)
1. Conduct pre-market scan  
2. Build watchlist  
3. Identify opening range and key levels  
4. Enter only on quant trigger + discretionary validation  
5. Set bracket orders immediately  
6. Manage with trailing or scaling  
7. Exit all trades by end-of-day  
8. Journal results

## 12. Strategy Objective
To create a **high-probability, low-risk, repeatable day-trading system** using a hybrid quant-discretion approach that is consistent, scalable, and risk-controlled.
